Noisy Blob

Made with: JavaScript, p5.js
Source: OpenProcessing

Noisy Blob was a sandbox toolkit I made as a project for CS44N: Great Ideas in Graphics. I used it to explore the properties of Perlin noise, which is essentially a controlled randomness that is used in things like semi-random terrain generation in video games. In this case, I use the noise to generate vertices that exist at varying lengths away from a radial center. This generates a "noisy blob", named Mr. Blobby.

Using the sliders, you can view how changing factors affects Mr. Blobby. Increasing the vertices should make him more circular, Changing the speed changes the rate at which new Perlin noise is generated, and increasing the noise factor normalizes the difference in vertice length (makes it more smooth).
