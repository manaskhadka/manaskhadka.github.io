Asteroid Escape

Made with: JavaScript, p5.js
Source: OpenProcessing

Asteroid Escape was a small game I made as a project for CS44N: Great Ideas in Graphics. It was an introductory exploration of expressing particles and particle interactions through pixel-based graphic representations. Particles are used here to represent asteroids, aesthetics (such as the UFO's propulsion), and even the UFO itself. Learning how to implement the physics of particles through code and optimizing efficiency for checking collisions was really cool.

I wanted to create an interactive piece of code with some sort of goal in mind for the user. The scope of the project was only to explore particles in graphics, but I hoped to make a game for my classmates to play and have fun with. I ended up with a game where you have to survive 30 seconds in the midst of an asteroid field. Can you make it?

Controls: Click for burst of height, move mouse to move UFO
